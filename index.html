Purpose of this document

  • Defines the assumptions underpinning planning, preparation and execution of a CiA Live transition
  • Sets the baseline for delivery. Both TechnologyOne and the customer rely on this throughout the project.
  • Any variation must be agreed in writing by both parties and may result in changes to scope, cost, or timeline
  • Applies only to the Core Enterprise Suite (CES) component of the CiA Live Migration
  • Should be read in conjunction with the CiA Live Migration Approach (CES) document
Section 1
Scope

What is in scope

Licensed modules per Contract As-is Ci footprint → CiA ETLs (last 12 months) XLOne reports & schedules (last 12 months) BI portlets & dashboards (last 12 months) Enquiries (last 12 months) Standard CiA roles and base roles

Where functionality has been reimagined in CiA, equivalent outcomes may be delivered through alternative features or approaches.

Explicitly excluded from scope

  • CiA capabilities already migrated by the customer or a third party
  • New features, products or modules
  • Changes to existing configuration or customisations
  • Integration with third-party systems or services
  • Application management or business administration services
  • Regression testing for application updates
  • Stored Scripts
  • Documents greater than 2,000 lines
  • DECOM scripts (format field validation)
  • SBR1 to SBR2 move
  • Multi-doc files with PO or Contract fields
  • Doc Ref 2 duplicate checking (Expense Management)
  • Unsupported workflow fields on entity definitions
  • Rework of migration activities unless T1 failed agreed requirements
TechnologyOne
  • Deliver migration per standard CiA Live Approach
  • Provide standard roles and base roles
  • Remote delivery of all services
  • Document improvements for separate post-project delivery
Customer
  • Provide accurate and complete information
  • Manage organisational change and user readiness
  • Complete UAT and validate all migrated artefacts
  • Provide access, resources and system admin support
  • Provide suitably skilled and available resources throughout

Duration guidance

  • Simple complexity: typically 4 to 6 months for CES migration
  • Simple means limited products, few CPM items, minimal variation from standard CiA
  • Complex environments with more products, integrations, or CPM items will extend beyond this range
  • Final duration confirmed after Discovery Stage completion and environment validation
  • Property and Rating migrations are planned separately and commence after CES migration completes.

Mobilisation and governance

  • A mobilisation period is required from Contract Start Date for T1 to allocate resources
  • Mobilisation re-applies if the project is paused and subsequently restarted
  • T1 confirms the allocated migration slot only once mobilisation is complete
  • Customer must formally review and approve the Migration Plan before execution begins
  • If Customer activities are delayed, T1 may proceed to subsequent stages. Any impacts will be managed via Change Control.
Section 3
Migration stage
TechnologyOne
  • Deliver migration per agreed Project Plan
  • Provide standard CiA Live roles and base roles
  • Progress to next stages if Customer activities are not completed in time
Customer
  • Complete all migration and testing activities within agreed timeframes
  • Complete UAT on all migrated items
  • System Administrator assigns roles to users
  • SMEs validate access during UAT
  • Confirm end users have appropriate access before completion
  • Actively participate and confirm readiness to progress at each stage

UAT requirements

  • Customer is responsible for UAT on all migrated items
  • Covers module functionality, reports, integrations, workflows, and analytics
  • Must be completed within the agreed timeframe to maintain the schedule
  • Extensions subject to resource availability and managed via Change Control

Access management

  • T1 provides standard roles and base roles within the provisioned environment
  • Customer System Administrator assigns roles to users
  • Customer SMEs validate access as part of UAT
  • Customer confirms end users have appropriate access prior to completion
Section 4
Change control

How change control works

  • Any variation to scope, schedule, or assumptions managed via Project Change Control Procedure
  • All changes must be formally raised, assessed, and agreed in writing before implementation
  • Applies to changes requested by either Customer or TechnologyOne
  • May result in adjustments to cost, timeline, or resource requirements

Customer obligations

  • Provide resources and complete activities within agreed timeframes
  • Supply accurate and timely information throughout the project
  • Cooperate and provide reasonable assistance including timely review of deliverables, participation in governance activities, and responses to information requests.
  • If obligations are not met, impacts are managed via the Change Control Procedure

Costs and delays

  • If the project is delayed, paused, or extended for reasons not caused by T1, additional costs may apply.
  • Includes extended environment access and additional consulting or project management effort
  • T1 standard daily rates per Contract apply to all approved additional work
  • Rescheduling or cancellation requires sufficient notice. Where notice is insufficient, T1 may recover committed resource time in line with the Contract.
Section 5
Other provisions

Travel

  • All services delivered remotely by default
  • Where on-site services are agreed, the customer is responsible for travel expenses including airfares and accommodation.
  • Travel time may be chargeable where contract thresholds are exceeded

Training

  • Primarily self-directed via T1 University and Customer Community
  • Each product includes a handover session of up to half a day following migration.
  • Handover sessions are not formal training and not intended for broad OCM activities
  • Customer responsible for end-user readiness, adoption and training
  • Additional formal training available via time and materials through Change Control

Integration

  • Customer responsible for all integration activities including third-party engagement
  • T1 only provides integration support where explicitly included in the Approach or agreed via Change Control

Project governance

  • Customer provides an experienced PM to work jointly with the T1 PM
  • Customer PM coordinates resources and ensures timely task completion
  • Governance includes regular status reporting, agreed meeting cadence, and documented outcomes
  • Additional governance requirements agreed during the Plan stage

Organisational change management

  • The customer is fully responsible for organisational change management including stakeholder engagement, communication, and end-user readiness.
  • OCM activities are not included within the scope of TechnologyOne services

General provisions

  • Defect resolution managed through standard support and maintenance arrangements
  • Project environments are provisioned for the duration defined in the Project Plan and are restricted to CiA Live activities only.
  • Standard shipped configuration used for roles, workplaces, and new modules unless otherwise agreed
  • Customer maintains any required third-party software licences
  • Migration tooling intended for desktop use and may have usage limitations
Section 6
Risks & acceptance gates
Production changes after scope sign-offAny post-sign-off changes are out of scope and must be redone after go-live at customer cost. A Production change freeze is strongly recommended.
Customer resource availabilityDelivery depends on suitably skilled and available customer resources. Delays may cause T1 to proceed to subsequent stages. Any impacts will be managed via Change Control.
Integration responsibility gapAll integrations with third-party systems are customer-owned. Insufficient planning is a frequent cause of project delays.
Unsupported Ci functionalityStored Scripts, documents over 2,000 lines, DECOM scripts, SBR1–SBR2, and others are explicitly out of scope and will not migrate.
Project pauses and restartsMobilisation re-applies if the project is paused and restarted. Additional costs may apply for extended environment access and effort.
Training and training and supportT1 University, Customer Community, and per-product handover sessions are available to support the customer. Formal OCM and end-user training remain the responsibility of the customer.
